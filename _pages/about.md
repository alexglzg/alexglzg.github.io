I am a robotics researcher. I believe robotics and autonomous systems have the potential to benefit countless applications. I am interested in developing robots that can increase safety and efficiency, and can reduce environmental harm, particularly in activities which may be dull or dangerous.

I am currently a Ph.D. researcher at KU Leuven, Belgium, and a member of the [MECO Research Team](https://www.mech.kuleuven.be/en/pma/research/meco), supervised by [Prof. Jan Swevers](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=X_fnO1YAAAAJ&hl=en). I received my B.S. degree in Mechatronics Engineering (2020), and my M.S. degree in Engineering (2022) from Tecnologico de Monterrey, Mexico. During my undergraduate studies, I completed a research stay at the [Aerospace Mechatronics Lab](https://aerospacemechatronics.lab.mcgill.ca/) from McGill University, with [Prof. Inna Sharf](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=Ys2mNPkAAAAJ&hl=en). The following year, I joined the [Robotics Group](https://tec.mx/en/research/where-research-carried-out/robotics) at Tecnologico de Monterrey, where I conducted both my undergraduate and master's research supervised by [Prof. Herman Castañeda](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=aAbtWIkAAAAJ&hl=en). Between 2021 and 2024, I was a recurrent visiting researcher for [CSAIL](https://www.csail.mit.edu/research/distributed-robotics-laboratory) and the [Senseable City Lab](https://senseable.mit.edu/) from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, wtih [Prof. Daniela Rus](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=910z20QAAAAJ&hl=en). My research interests include robust control and safe motion planning applied to autonomous systems in water, ground and air.
